PUSH IT! Venturing in a Food Cart Business

Aillie Cajucom
Good 0 \ Bad 0 \ Comments 0

Reads (619) | July 01, 2013

Want to manage a business with quite a small capital? Possible! With opportunities of franchising or even putting up your own food cart or kiosk business, running a business have become easier and more feasible nowadays.

 

However, RISK is always part of the battle plan. No matter how small or big investment is being released, there is really no assurance that the profits would go high easily or speedily.

 

To assist you on your quest of opening your food cart, EYP brings you these tips:

 

 

1. Find your passion. Aside the primary goal of earning, it will always be better that you have knowledge of what kind of business you are planning to build or product to sell. Best to choose the stuff you're fond of doing, remember what Confucius said?

 

 

 

 

 

2. Feasibility check. Go on a research, observe the current market trends, find a strategic location, and if franchising, don't settle for just one—compare packages and attend entrep fairs (Don't forget to try out their products too!).

 

 

 

 

3. Creativity. Of course you will need to be creative with your product! Chances are, your business or product may not be the first of its kind, nor the only one in the market. Think of ways on how to standout among the rest.

 

 

 

You're business savvy. But are you DIY savvy? Here's a list of basic tools that every toolbox should have.

 

 

 

4. Careful costing. Keep in mind the budget that you are allocating for your initial investment. If franchising, starting small could be the best option. On the other hand, if trying to start on your own, maximize available resources, start with your own appliances and situate it on your own place perhaps.

 

 

 

 

 

5. Finalize. After your feasibility check, settle your business as with the location (rent), the food cart purchase, and start looking for prospective personnel. You can do this step back-to-back with number 6.

 

 

6. Register. Make your business legal by getting the necessary permits from your barangay or municipality. You may also need to get a sanitary permit, especially if the products are food. Get DTI-registered so you'll have ownership of your business name.

 

 

 

 

 

7. Procure, hire, and test drive. As soon as things have been sorted out and little by little, the most essential parts of the business are in their proper places, it's time to stock your supplies for about a week or two and hire that most qualified crewmember and orient him/her in the daily work. Go for a soft launch and promote your products in an introductory pricing.

 

 

8. Conceptualize. Business does not end in just opening it. You will need to always find a way for it to maintain (or improve) its standing. Never stop creating promotions and advertisements to capture more market and make your products known. Online tools are very powerful now, and of course, your print ads and word-of-mouth marketing will play great parts.

 

 

Last tip: add some personal touch. Check on your customers and your crew; they will always feel special knowing that the owner is giving them attention!

 

 

Good luck to your business!
